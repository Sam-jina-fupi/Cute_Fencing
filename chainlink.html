FENCE TYPES

Chainlink Fences

Chainlink fences, also known as wire-mesh fences or cyclone fences, are a popular type of fencing widely used for various purposes. They are constructed using woven or interlocked steel wires that form a diamond-shaped pattern. Chainlink fences offer several advantages, including affordability, durability, and versatility.

Chainlink/Wooden Poles Fences


Wooden fences are popular for their natural beauty, versatility, and privacy. They are available in different styles, including picket, privacy, and split rail fences.

Picket Fences


Picket fences are a classic and charming type of fencing commonly associated with residential properties. They are characterized by their traditional design, featuring evenly spaced vertical wooden boards called pickets that are attached to horizontal rails. Picket fences offer both aesthetic appeal and functional benefits.

Picket(Wooden) Fences


A picket wooden fence is a classic and popular type of fencing that features vertical wooden boards called pickets attached to horizontal rails.

Picket (Concrete) Fences


A concrete fence with picket-style inserts combines the durability and strength of concrete with the aesthetic appeal of picket fencing. In this design, precast concrete panels or posts are used, and sections of the fence have openings or cutouts that resemble traditional pickets.

Electric Fences

Electric wire fences, commonly known as electric fences, are a specialized type of fencing system that utilizes electric shocks to deter and control animals or intruders. They are commonly used for perimeter security and agricultural applications.

Electric Perimeter Wall Fences

Electric wire fences offer an effective deterrent and detection system for securing the perimeter of properties. They provide a powerful shock upon contact, discouraging trespassers and intruders from attempting to breach the fence. Electric fences can be customized to meet specific security requirements, including the number of wires, height, and voltage.

Electric Agricultural Fences

Electric wire fences are widely used in agricultural settings for containing livestock and protecting crops. They provide a cost-effective solution for establishing boundaries and preventing animals from entering or leaving designated areas. Electric fences are adaptable to different terrains and can be easily moved or modified as needed.

Razor Wire Fences

Razor wire fences are a type of security fencing that incorporates sharp-edged blades or barbs to create an additional layer of deterrence and protection. They are commonly used in high-security areas where enhanced perimeter security is required.

Flat Razor Wire Fence

Flat razor wire fences consist of sharp-edged metal strips that are connected in a flat, ribbon-like configuration. The metal strips are typically made of galvanized steel or stainless steel and have sharp, pointed edges. They are designed to deter and impede unauthorized access by creating a formidable physical barrier.

Concertina/Round Razor

Round razor wire fences, also known as concertina wire, consist of wire coils with sharp barbs or blades attached at regular intervals. The wire used in round razor wire fences is usually galvanized or stainless steel. The coils are formed into a helical or concertina shape, with the sharp barbs pointing outwards.
